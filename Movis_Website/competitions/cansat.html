Designing and building satellite prototypes that fit in a soda can for atmospheric research

About the Competition

The CANSAT competition challenges student teams to design, build, and launch a functioning satellite the size of a soda can. This international competition, often organized by space agencies and educational institutions, provides hands-on experience in space technology development on a small scale.

Teams must create a complete space mission, including the satellite's design, payload development, launch preparation, and data analysis, all while adhering to strict size and weight constraints.
